Tragedy struck a railway station in southwestern Pakistan as a powerful explosion ripped through the booking office, claiming the lives of at least 17 people and leaving 46 injured. The blast occurred shortly before the arrival of the Jaffer Express, which was scheduled to depart for Peshawar.
At least 17 people were killed and 46 injured after an explosion at a railway station in southwestern Pakistan, Dawn News reported. The train involved was reportedly traveling from Quetta to Peshawar. Police and rescue teams are currently at the scene.
Initial reports suggest that the explosion occurred at the booking office of the railway station, just moments before the Jaffer Express was due to arrive at the platform, according to Geo News.
"The death toll has now risen to 17, and it could climb further," said Inspector General Moazzam Jah Ansari in an interview with AFP.
The train, which was set to depart for Peshawar at 9.00am, had not yet reached the platform when the blast took place, PTI reported quoting railway officials.
In addition, a spokesperson for the local hospital confirmed that 46 people injured in the explosion have been admitted for treatment.
"The blast took place inside the railway station when the Peshawar-bound express was about to leave for its destination," Muhammad Baloch, senior superintendent of police operations, told Reuters.
In a statement, Shahid Rind, spokesperson for the Balochistan government, confirmed that both police and security forces had arrived at the scene of the explosion. He said that the authorities were actively investigating the nature of the blast. The Bomb Disposal Squad was on-site, meticulously gathering evidence to determine the cause of the incident.
